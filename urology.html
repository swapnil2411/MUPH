What we do

A urologist is trained to diagnose and treat problems of the urinary tract. Urologists deal with men and women alike. With women, a urologist deals only with the problems of the urinary tract.
Female urology covers a set of conditions such as overactive bladder, urinary incontinence, and pelvic organ prolapse which are treated by a female urologist or urogynecologist. With men, however, the urologist can diagnose and treat disorders of the penis and testicles also. They also look after male infertility and sexual disorders.
If you get an appointment with the urologist, they will generally take notes regarding your condition from the referring doctor during their first visit. They will examine your medical history and do the physical examination in the following phase.
During the examination, the patients are asked to undress from the waist down and are provided with a gown so that the urologist can easily examine the exact issue.

Subspecialty Areas of Urology

The scope of urology is significant as it possesses various sub-specialty areas like

Urologic Oncology: Leading the Fight Against Urological Cancers

Our urologic oncology specialists are at the forefront of diagnosing and treating urological cancers. With cutting-edge techniques and a multidisciplinary approach, we provide personalized care for kidney, bladder, prostate, testicular, and adrenal gland cancers. We are dedicated to delivering the highest standard of care to patients and advancing the field through ongoing research.

Endourology: Minimally Invasive Solutions for Urological Conditions

Our endourologists specialize in minimally invasive procedures to diagnose and treat urological conditions. Using state-of-the-art technology, we offer advanced treatments for kidney stones, ureteral obstructions, and other urinary tract disorders. Our focus on less invasive techniques ensures faster recovery times and improved patient outcomes.

Female Urology: Compassionate Care for Women's Urological Health

Our female urology specialists understand the unique urological needs of women. We provide comprehensive care for issues such as urinary incontinence, pelvic organ prolapse, and pelvic floor disorders. Our patient-centered approach ensures that women receive tailored treatments and the support they need for improved quality of life.

Pediatric Urology: Nurturing the Health of Our Youngest Patients

Our pediatric urology experts specialize in caring for the urological needs of children. From congenital abnormalities to bedwetting and urinary tract infections, our team is dedicated to providing gentle, effective, and child-friendly treatments. We ensure that our young patients receive the specialized care they deserve in a warm and supportive environment.

Reconstructive Urology: Restoring Health and Functionality

Our reconstructive urology specialists are skilled in surgical procedures that restore or reconstruct the urinary tract system. Whether it's repairing structural issues or addressing complications from previous surgeries, we are committed to helping patients regain health and functionality.

Neurourology: Addressing Nerve-Related Urological Conditions

Our neurourologists specialize in diagnosing and treating urological conditions caused by nerve dysfunction. We provide expert care for neurogenic bladder and other neurologic disorders affecting the urinary system. Our goal is to improve bladder function and enhance the quality of life for our patients.

Andrology: Enhancing Male Reproductive Health

Our andrology specialists focus on the health and well-being of the male reproductive system. We offer personalized solutions for issues such as erectile dysfunction, infertility, and male hormonal disorders. Our comprehensive approach ensures that men receive the support and treatments they need to maintain optimal reproductive health.

Transplant Urology: Transforming Lives Through Kidney Transplants

Our transplant urologists are dedicated to kidney transplantation, providing expert care from donor evaluation to post-transplant follow-up. We are committed to transforming lives through successful kidney transplants and enhancing the quality of life for transplant recipients.

Laparoscopic and Robotic Urology: Advancing Surgical Techniques

Our laparoscopic and robotic urology specialists are pioneers in minimally invasive surgical techniques. With precision and innovation, we perform procedures such as prostatectomy and kidney surgery using advanced technology. Our focus on less invasive approaches ensures shorter recovery times and improved patient outcomes.

Voiding Dysfunction: Restoring Bladder Function

Our specialists in voiding dysfunction diagnose and treat disorders related to bladder function and urinary flow. From urinary retention to overactive bladder, we offer expert care to restore optimal bladder function and improve quality of life.

Men's Health: Holistic Care for Men's Well-Being

Our men's health urologists provide comprehensive care for men's well-being, addressing issues such as sexual dysfunction, testosterone deficiency, and overall health. We prioritize men's unique health needs and offer personalized solutions to support their vitality and quality of life.

What Can I Expect From a Urologist?

  • The urologist will normally perform a range of tests to determine what the problem is. Imaging tests, such as an ultrasound, CT, Cystoscopy, MRI scan, can help the urologist to locate the problem and a urine test may be performed to check for bacteria and other signs of disease.
  • A patient may feel uncomfortable during cystoscopy, but it does not produce disturbing pain. The most commonly ordered tests in urology are urine collection and blood tests. They are generally one of the initial steps while diagnosing and screening urologic conditions.
  • If the situation is more serious, a biopsy may be performed; this is traditionally to check for cancer and other disorders. The urologist may also ask you questions about your medical history and carry out a physical examination.
  • If you’re experiencing a frequent need to urinate, pain in passing urine, difficulty in passing urine, blood in urine, pain in lower back, or trouble sustaining an erection and early ejaculation. You should consult an expert. In order to maintain a healthy urinary tract, urologists recommend that you regularly empty your bladder and drink water instead of caffeine.
  • They also stress the importance of avoiding smoking and maintaining a low-salt diet. By following these simple rules you can prevent a wide range of urological problems. All tumours or cancer of the kidney, prostate, penis, urinary bladder and testis are also treated by the urologist. Moreover, Most urologists also do kidney transplants.

What Kind of Education & Training Does a Urologist Have?

Urologists must have a medical degree from a certified institution. They can join a medical college after clearing the NEET examination. To prepare for a urology examination or NEET, one must stand well-versed in subjects like physics, chemistry, and biology. After medical school, urologists undergo a residency in a urology facility. After five years, the student must pass a specialist board test. After residency and board tests, urologists can work at hospitals or create their own offices.

When Should I See The Urologist?

Your doctor may advise you to visit a urologist if you’re exhibiting symptoms of a Urinary Tract Infection (UTI), difficulty in passing urine, blood in urine, Incontinence (urinary leakage), or Erectile Dysfunction. As these illnesses lie in the scope of a urologist, a general doctor will recommend that you visit one. The urologist also looks after male infertility and sexual problems.

Conclusion

To prevent the diseases mentioned above, doctors advise staying hydrated and holding pee for long durations as it can severely affect your health. Maintaining hygiene while excreting and proper sanitization is a general rule of thumb. In case of severe or critical conditions, consult well-experienced faculty at the urology dept at MPUH.

In most cases, urology exams are painless. But, your symptoms and pain threshold will be the best judge of whether the exams are painless or not.

Yes, even women can see a urologist. After all, the urinary tract is different from the reproductive system of girls and women.
