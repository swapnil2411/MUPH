About JPAC

The Jayaramdas Patel Academic Centre (JPAC) was established in 2007 to bridge the gap between expected standards of Endourology and Laparoscopic Surgery, as well as existing facilities of training in the Indian subcontinent. Named after the founder of MPUH, the center functions on the concept of ‘Enter to Learn, Exit to Serve’– one that keeps up with the latest trends and provides an array of training opportunities to students.

Over the years, JPAC has evolved into a vibrant center for learning and advancement, offering a plethora of workshops, training & seminars. The training & learning programs are conducted by leading National and International faculty and garner wide participation from India as well as overseas. In 1993, MPUH started a full-fledged Diploma of National Board of Examinations super specialty program in Urology and Nephrology- a game changer that not only made the atmosphere academic and scientific-evidence based, but also enhanced the hospital’s standard of care. Results of research work were published in national and international journals and recognition poured in from across the world. Two medical world congresses organized by the hospital became runaway success-inspiring Team MPUH to finally establish the Jayaramdas Patel Academic Centre (JPAC) for teaching and training in 2007. By virtue of having adopted the most modern methods of treatment, the hospital had a large repository of clinical volumes and the finest physicians who had also conducted research in new areas of their specialties.
